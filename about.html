I work best where ambiguity needs structure.

I design with a sociologist’s brain and a storyteller’s instincts. My work is shaped by accessibility, systems thinking, visual communication, and a long-running interest in how people move through complicated environments.

I am most useful at the beginning of a problem, when the work still feels messy, abstract, or half-formed. I like clarifying what something should be, how it should function, who it needs to support, and why it matters.

Structure

I turn ambiguity into usable frameworks, whether that means organizing content, mapping user needs, clarifying a workflow, or shaping a product concept into something people can understand.

Access

I care about design that accounts for disability, stress, fatigue, context, and constraint. Real people are not frictionless stock photos, which is rude of them but important for design.

Story

My creative background helps me think about tone, narrative, visual clarity, and emotional logic. Good design does not just function; it communicates.

Right now, I am focused on the intersection of product design, accessibility, UX research, and human systems engineering. I am drawn to work that requires careful thinking, collaboration, iteration, and a refusal to treat people like edge cases.
